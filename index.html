// Prep & Recovery
WARM-UP &
RECOVERY
Morning routine · 5 min pre-workout · 5 min post-workout cooldown · Day-specific stretches
🌅
MORNING ROUTINE
7:00–7:30 AM · Do these immediately after waking up · 15 min total
Why this matters: You train at 8 AM. Your body needs ~60 min from wake-up to be ready for heavy lifting. This routine primes your nervous system, hydrates you, and gets blood moving.
1
Drink 2 glasses of water
You're dehydrated from 7–8 hrs of sleep. Do this before anything — before phone, before bathroom.
30 sec
2
Sun exposure
Step outside or near a window for 5 min. Natural light sets your circadian rhythm and kills morning grogginess.
5 min
3
Dead Hang or Doorframe Stretch
Hang from a bar or grip a doorframe overhead and lean back. Decompresses spine after sleeping. 3×20–30 sec.
2 min
4
Cat-Cow (10 reps)
On all fours. Arch back up like a cat, then drop belly and look up like a cow. Slow and controlled. Mobilises your entire spine.
1 min
5
World's Greatest Stretch
Step forward into lunge, plant same-side hand down, rotate upper body and reach to ceiling. 5 reps each side. Hits hip flexors, thoracic spine, hamstrings all at once.
2 min
6
Eat Pre-Workout Meal
Oats + 2 eggs + banana. Eat this 45–60 min before hitting the gym. Don't skip — training fasted burns muscle, not just fat.
7:30 AM
⚡
PRE-WORKOUT WARM-UP
At the gym · Do before every session · 5 min total · Day-specific
Rule: Never lift cold. 5 min of this prevents 5 weeks of injury. The goal is to raise heart rate, increase joint temperature, and wake up the specific muscles you're about to train.
Step 1 — Universal (2 min, every day)
1
Jump Rope or Jog on spot
1–2 min continuous. Gets heart rate up, lubricates joints, warms core temperature. If no rope, high knees for 60 sec.
60–90 sec
2
Arm Circles + Shoulder Rolls
10 forward, 10 backward arm circles. Then 10 shoulder rolls each direction. Essential before any upper body day.
30 sec
Step 2 — Day-Specific Activation (3 min)
1
Band Pull-Aparts (or towel)
Hold a band or towel wide, pull apart to open chest. 15 reps. Activates rear delts, opens chest before bench.
15 reps
2
Wall Slides
Back against wall, arms at 90°. Slide arms overhead keeping contact with wall. Fixes shoulder mechanics before pressing.
10 reps
3
Light Bench / Push-up Ramp
Do 1 set push-ups × 10, then 1 warm-up set on bench at 40–50% of your working weight × 12. Never jump straight to heavy.
1 set each
1
Dead Hang
Hang from pull-up bar with full grip, relax completely. Decompresses spine, opens lats and shoulder girdle. 2×20 sec.
2×20 sec
2
Scapular Pull-ups
Hang from bar. Without bending elbows, depress and retract shoulder blades to rise slightly. 10 reps. Activates mid-back before rows.
10 reps
3
Light Cable Row or Band Row
1 warm-up set at very light weight × 15. Primes the pulling pattern before loading. Don't skip this on deadlift day.
1 warm-up set
1
Glute Bridges
Lie on back, feet flat. Drive hips up and squeeze glutes hard at top. 2×15 reps. Wakes up glutes before squats — most people have dormant glutes.
2×15 reps
2
Leg Swings
Hold wall for balance. Swing leg forward and back 10×, then side to side 10×. Each leg. Opens hip flexors and hip joint.
10×10 each leg
3
Bodyweight Squat Ramp
10 slow bodyweight squats focusing on depth and knee tracking. Then 1 bar-only squat set × 10. Never skip this — knees need it.
10 + 10 reps
🧊
POST-WORKOUT COOLDOWN
After every session · 5 min max · Reduces soreness, improves flexibility over time
Why stretch after, not before? Static stretching before training reduces strength output. Do it after when muscles are warm — that's when you actually improve flexibility. Holding each stretch 30–45 sec is the minimum to have any effect.
Universal Cooldown — Every Day (5 min)
1
Child's Pose
Kneel, sit back on heels, arms extended forward on floor. Breathe deeply. Decompresses lower back, stretches lats. Hold 45 sec.
45 sec
2
Hip Flexor Stretch (Kneeling Lunge)
One knee on floor, other foot forward. Push hips forward and hold. Critical — you sit a lot studying, hip flexors are always tight. 30 sec each side.
30 sec × 2
3
Hamstring Stretch (Standing or Seated)
Sit on floor, legs straight. Reach toward toes. Don't bounce — just hold. Essential after legs day and RDLs. 30–45 sec.
45 sec
4
Cross-body Shoulder Stretch
Pull one arm across chest with other arm. Hold. Essential after every upper body day to prevent shoulder tightness. 20 sec each side.
20 sec × 2
5
Chest Doorframe Stretch
Stand in doorway, arms at 90°, lean forward. Opens chest that gets tight from bench pressing. If you skip this daily, you'll get hunched posture. 30 sec.
30 sec
6
Deep Breathing (Box Breath)
Inhale 4 sec → hold 4 sec → exhale 4 sec → hold 4 sec. 5 cycles. Brings down heart rate, signals nervous system to shift to recovery mode.
2 min
🧴 EXTRA — IF YOU HAVE A FOAM ROLLER
Roll your quads, IT band, upper back — 30–60 sec per area. Breaks up fascia, reduces DOMS (post-workout soreness). Do it on Sunday especially during active recovery. Not mandatory but it makes a noticeable difference by week 3+.
